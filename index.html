History of Robotics The history of robotics dates back to ancient times, with myths and legends featuring automata. However, the modern field began in the 20th century. Key milestones include:

  • 1950s: The first programmable robot, Unimate, was developed by George Devol and later used in General Motors factories for assembly line work.
  • 1970s: The introduction of more sophisticated robotic arms, which allowed for greater precision and versatility in manufacturing processes.
  • 1980s: Advancements in AI and computer technology led to robots that could learn and adapt, enhancing their capabilities beyond mere automation.
  • 2000s-Present: The emergence of collaborative robots (cobots), drones, and autonomous vehicles, making robots accessible in various sectors, including healthcare, agriculture, and transportation.
